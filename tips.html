💡 Nutrition Tips & Education

Simple, practical advice to help you eat healthier every day

🌟

Tip of the Day

Swap your afternoon bubble tea for green tea or water with lemon. A regular bubble tea can have up to 500 calories and 50g of sugar — that's more than a can of Coke!

🥗

Fill Half Your Plate with Vegetables

Aim to fill at least half your plate with vegetables at every meal. They're low in calories but high in vitamins, minerals, and fibre that keep you full longer.

Balanced Diet
💧

Drink Water Before Meals

Drinking a glass of water 30 minutes before eating helps with digestion and can prevent overeating. Many times, thirst is mistaken for hunger.

Hydration
🧠

Eat Slowly and Mindfully

It takes about 20 minutes for your brain to register that you're full. Put your phone down, chew slowly, and enjoy your food to avoid overeating.

Mindful Eating
🍗

Choose Grilled Over Fried

Grilled chicken has about 165 calories per serving, while fried chicken can have over 300. Same protein, much less fat — an easy swap at any hawker centre.

Protein
🧃

Watch Hidden Sugars in Drinks

A kopi with condensed milk has about 14g of sugar. Try kopi-o kosong (black coffee) or ask for less sugar. Small changes add up over the week.

Sugar & Sodium
🌾

Switch to Brown Rice

Brown rice has 3x more fibre than white rice and keeps you fuller longer. Many stalls now offer brown rice — just ask! Your energy levels will thank you.

Fibre
🍱

Plan Your Meals Ahead

Spending 5 minutes deciding what to eat before you're hungry helps avoid impulsive unhealthy choices. Try planning tomorrow's meals tonight.

Balanced Diet
🧂

Ask for Less Sauce

Sauces and gravies can add 200-400mg of sodium per tablespoon. Ask for sauce on the side or request "less gravy" — your blood pressure will thank you.

Sugar & Sodium
🥚

Add an Egg for Protein

Eggs are one of the cheapest protein sources (about $0.30 each). Adding a boiled egg to your meal increases protein by 6g and helps you stay full until your next meal.

Protein

🍔 Fast Food & Local Swaps

Common questions about making healthier choices when eating out

Skip the fries and soda — swap for grilled chicken burger with water. Saves 400+ calories. Choose single patty, add extra lettuce, and ask for sauce on the side.

Order thin crust, load up on veggie toppings, limit to 2 slices with a side salad. Avoid stuffed crust and processed meats like pepperoni.

Ask for less sambal (reduces sodium), choose grilled chicken over fried, add extra cucumber, and request less coconut rice. Saves 150-200 calories.

Order 0% or 25% sugar, swap pearls for aloe vera or konjac jelly, and choose fresh milk over non-dairy creamer. Best: iced green tea with aloe (60 kcal vs 440 kcal).

Choose soup-based over fried dishes, add vegetables, pick lean proteins (fish, chicken breast), avoid oily rice, and drink plain water instead of sweet drinks.
