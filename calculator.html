Understanding Your Numbers

Science-backed calculations using established medical formulas.

📏

BMI (Asian Cutoffs)

We use WHO's Asian BMI cutoffs (Underweight <18.5, Normal 18.5–22.9, Overweight ≥23, Obese ≥27.5) as recommended by HPB Singapore — Asian populations face higher metabolic risks at lower BMI than Western populations.

🔥

Mifflin-St Jeor Equation

Your BMR is calculated using the Mifflin-St Jeor equation (1990), recommended by the Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics as the most accurate formula. TDEE = BMR × activity factor.

🥗

Macro Split (HPB Guidelines)

Macros follow the IOM Acceptable Macronutrient Distribution Ranges and HPB Singapore's "My Healthy Plate": 25% protein, 50% carbohydrates, 25% fats — balanced for active students.

📚

Scientific Sources

• Mifflin MD et al. Am J Clin Nutr. 1990;51(2):241-247
• WHO Expert Consultation. Lancet. 2004;363(9403)
• HPB Singapore BMI Guidelines
• IOM Dietary Reference Intakes, 2005

⚖️

Weight Change Science

1 kg of body fat ≈ 7,700 kcal (Wishnofsky, 1958). A 500 kcal/day deficit produces ~0.45 kg/week loss. We enforce minimum intakes (1,500 kcal for males, 1,200 for females) per NHS and HPB safety guidelines.

⚠️

Important Disclaimer

These are estimates based on population averages. Individual metabolism, genetics, and health conditions cause variation. For medical or dietary conditions, please consult a qualified healthcare professional.
